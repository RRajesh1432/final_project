HOW IT WORKS — 4 STEPS
From raw audio to threat verdict in under 10 seconds
1
INPUT
🎵
Audio Input
Upload any audio file (WAV, MP3, FLAC, OGG, M4A, WebM) or record live from your microphone. The system supports continuous live monitoring in 10-second chunks.
2
PROCESS
📊
Spectrogram Conversion
Audio is converted to a Log-Mel Spectrogram using a 25ms Hanning window, 10ms hop, and 128 mel frequency bands. This turns sound into an image the AI can read.
3
CLASSIFY
🤖
AST Inference
The Audio Spectrogram Transformer (partially fine-tuned on threat sounds) classifies the audio across all 527 AudioSet categories using sigmoid probabilities — not softmax.
4
DECIDE
⚡
Threat Decision
Score_t(x) = max probability across the 5 threat classes. If score ≥ τ (0.75), or a threat class ranks in the top 5 predictions — THREAT is flagged and ntfy alert fires instantly.

HOW TO READ THE THREAT SCORE
Score_t(x) = max probability across all 5 threat classes
0–29%
SAFE
No threat class detected. Audio is likely speech, music, ambient noise, or other non-threatening sound.
30–74%
MEDIUM
Possible threat pattern. Could be a muffled or distant threat sound, or an ambiguous recording. Manual review recommended.
75–100%
HIGH ⚠
Score_t ≥ τ (0.75). Strong match to a threat class. ntfy alert fires immediately. Treat as real threat until confirmed otherwise.
ⓘ Three detection rules run in parallel: Score threshold (score ≥ τ), Top-rank (threat class in top 5 with score >0.15), and Top-2 (threat class is #1 or #2 prediction with score >0.10). Any one rule triggering fires an alert.
UNDERSTANDING YOUR RESULTS
What each section in the Analyze page shows you
Threat Score Gauge
The circular gauge shows Score_t — the max probability across all 5 threat classes. The τ marker at 75% is the paper-validated detection threshold.
AI Explanation
Claude Haiku analyses the result and tells you in plain English what was heard, whether it's dangerous, and what action to take.
Log-Mel Spectrogram
Visual representation of the audio. Bright bands at specific frequencies are the patterns the AI uses. Gunshots show sharp vertical lines; sirens show sweeping curves.
Temporal Segments
The audio is split into 5 time windows and each is classified independently. This shows when in the recording the threat event occurred.
Threat Class Bars
Shows probability for each of the 5 threat classes (gunshot, explosion, glass, scream, siren). The highest bar drove the detection decision.
Top AudioSet Classes
The top 12 predictions across all 527 AudioSet sound categories, sorted by confidence. Useful for understanding what non-threat sounds were present.
